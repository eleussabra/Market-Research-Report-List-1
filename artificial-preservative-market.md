<p><p>The artificial preservative market consists of various types, including sorbates, benzoates, propionates, nitrites, sulfates, and others. Sorbates, often used in food and beverages, inhibit mold and yeast growth. Benzoates serve as strong antimicrobial agents, while propionates are effective against mold in baked goods. Nitrites are primarily used in cured meats to prevent bacterial growth and enhance color. Sulfates function as antioxidants and preservatives in various products. Each type contributes uniquely to extending shelf life and maintaining product safety.</p></p>
