<p><p>The tellurium dioxide market includes two primary forms: α-TeO2 and β-TeO2. α-TeO2 is often used in ceramics, glass, and optical applications due to its stability and high refractive index. In contrast, β-TeO2 exhibits unique optical and electrical properties, making it suitable for photonic devices and as a nonlinear optical material. Both forms find applications across various industries, including electronics, telecommunications, and material sciences, driven by the demand for advanced materials with specific functionalities.</p></p>
