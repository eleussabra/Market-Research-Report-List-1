<p><p>Silk sheets come in several types, each derived from different silk sources. Mulberry silk is the most luxurious, known for its softness and durability, produced by silkworms fed exclusively on mulberry leaves. Tussah silk, made from wild silkworms, has a coarser texture and a natural beige color. Castor silk, derived from castor bean plants, offers an eco-friendly alternative, while cassava silk, made from the cassava plant, presents a sustainable, biodegradable option, catering to diverse consumer preferences and environmental concerns.</p></p>
