<p><p>Submarine electricity transmission systems utilize various cable types to transfer power underwater. Mass-impregnated cables contain an insulating material that enhances electrical performance and durability. Self-contained fluid-filled cables use a pressurized fluid for insulation and cooling, offering superior thermal properties. Extruded insulation cables are made with solid polymer insulation, providing flexibility and resistance to moisture. Other market types include specialized cables for unique applications, such as high-voltage direct current (HVDC) systems, tailored to meet specific operational requirements in challenging marine environments.</p></p>
